Knowledge Guide

Background of the Project

The Global Communities' Septic Tank Calculator was developed to address a critical gap in sustainable wastewater management. Poorly designed septic systems pose significant health and environmental risks, especially in resource-limited settings. This tool provides a robust framework for designing septic tanks and leach fields based on precise engineering calculations.

Goals of the Project

  • Provide an intuitive interface for septic tank design calculations.
  • Incorporate real-world factors such as soil type, groundwater levels, and effluent reuse.
  • Promote environmental sustainability and public health through proper wastewater management.

How the Tool Works

The tool calculates septic tank dimensions and leach field areas using **engineering formulas** and **standard constants** derived from environmental and wastewater management guidelines. Here's a breakdown of the calculations:

  • Volume of Liquid: This is based on the daily water usage per household member, flush count, and cistern size. The formula is: Volume of Liquid = Household Size × Flush Count × Cistern Size.
  • Volume of Sludge: The tool assumes a sludge accumulation rate per person and calculates the total sludge volume.
  • Total Volume: The total tank volume combines the liquid and sludge volumes, multiplied by a retention time multiplier: Total Volume = (Volume of Liquid + Volume of Sludge) × Retention Time.
  • Leach Field Area: The leach field area is determined by soil percolation rate and total effluent volume. The formula includes a multiplier for effluent reuse and seasonal flooding risks.

Constants like sludge accumulation rate, flush count, cistern size, and percolation rates are pre-defined based on standard environmental guidelines and can be adjusted for local contexts.

Technical Details

The backend of the tool is powered by Flask, a lightweight Python web framework. Inputs from the user are validated and passed to a set of functions in the `calculations.py` file. These functions use predefined constants from `constants.py` to compute the septic tank dimensions, H₂S emissions, and leach field area.

The formulas and constants used in the calculations are grounded in standard engineering principles, such as:

  • Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) guidelines.
  • WHO standards for wastewater management.
  • Site-specific adjustments for effluent reuse and flooding risks.

The results are rendered dynamically on the Results page, and the tool uses a responsive design for usability on both desktop and mobile devices.
